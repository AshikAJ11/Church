Roman Catholic Festivals in 2025

Solemnity of Mary, Mother of God

January 1, 2025
This solemnity, a Holy Day of Obligation, celebrates Mary as the Mother of God, emphasizing her role in the Incarnation. Catholics attend Mass to honor her divine motherhood, with the Gloria and Credo recited. The feast reflects on Mary's fiat and her significance in salvation history.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://www.easterbrooks.com/personal/cal_v2/yearly.html)

Epiphany of the Lord

January 5, 2025
Commemorates the visit of the Magi to the infant Jesus, symbolizing His manifestation to the Gentiles. Celebrated with special Masses, the blessing of chalk, and house blessings in some traditions. In the U.S., it’s observed on the Sunday between January 2 and 8.[](https://www.cbcew.org.uk/religious-festivals/)[](https://www.easterbrooks.com/personal/cal_v2/yearly.html)

Ash Wednesday

March 5, 2025
Marks the start of Lent, a 40-day period of penance. Catholics receive ashes on their foreheads as a sign of repentance, with the words, “Repent and believe in the Gospel.” Fasting and abstinence are observed, and Mass is attended.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://www.quora.com/What-festivals-do-Roman-Catholics-celebrate)

Palm Sunday

April 13, 2025
Begins Holy Week, commemorating Jesus’ triumphal entry into Jerusalem. Palms are blessed and distributed, often carried in processions. The Passion narrative is read during Mass, preparing Catholics for the events of Holy Week.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Holy Thursday

April 17, 2025
Celebrates the Last Supper, where Jesus instituted the Eucharist and the priesthood. The Mass of the Lord’s Supper includes the washing of feet, symbolizing service. The Eucharist is processed to an altar of repose for adoration.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Good Friday

April 18, 2025
A day of fasting and penance commemorating Jesus’ crucifixion. The liturgy includes the Passion reading, veneration of the Cross, and Communion from pre-consecrated hosts. Churches are stripped bare, reflecting solemnity.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Easter Sunday

April 20, 2025
The pinnacle of the liturgical year, celebrating Jesus’ Resurrection. The Easter Vigil and Sunday Masses feature the Gloria, renewal of baptismal vows, and festive liturgies. Easter eggs and meals symbolize new life.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Ascension of the Lord

May 29, 2025
Marks Jesus’ ascension into heaven 40 days after Easter. Often transferred to the following Sunday in many dioceses (June 1, 2025, in the U.S.). Masses include the Gloria and focus on Christ’s exaltation.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Pentecost Sunday

June 8, 2025
Celebrates the descent of the Holy Spirit on the Apostles, marking the birth of the Church. Red vestments are worn, and the Sequence is sung. Masses emphasize empowerment for mission.[](https://www.diversityresources.com/interfaith-calendar-2025/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Feast of Corpus Christi

June 19, 2025
Honors the Eucharist as the Body and Blood of Christ. Celebrated with Masses and processions carrying the Blessed Sacrament. In some regions, it’s moved to the following Sunday (June 22, 2025).[](https://factsanddetails.com/world/cat55/sub392/entry-5812.html)[](https://www.diversityresources.com/interfaith-calendar-2025/)

Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary

August 15, 2025
A Holy Day of Obligation celebrating Mary’s assumption into heaven. Masses include the Gloria and focus on her role as Queen of Heaven. Local traditions may include processions and Marian devotions.[](https://www.diversityresources.com/interfaith-calendar-2025/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

All Saints’ Day

November 1, 2025
A Holy Day of Obligation honoring all saints, known and unknown. Catholics attend Mass to celebrate the communion of saints. The day encourages reflection on holiness and martyrdom.[](https://factsanddetails.com/world/cat55/sub392/entry-5812.html)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)

Christmas

December 25, 2025
Celebrates the Nativity of Jesus, a Holy Day of Obligation. Masses include the Christmas Vigil, Midnight Mass, and Dawn/Day Masses, with the Gloria and festive decorations. Advent precedes it with four weeks of preparation.[](https://icalendars.net/religion/catholicism/)[](https://catholicism.website/catholicism/a-guide-to-catholic-holidays-and-festivals/)
